Early Life and Career Beginnings

Dave Attell was born in Queens, New York, and developed an interest in comedy at a young age. He attended New York University, where he graduated with a degree in communications. After graduation, Attell began performing stand-up comedy at open mic nights in New York City, gradually building a reputation for his edgy humor and unique delivery.

Attell’s big break came when he was featured on “The Late Show with David Letterman.” His performance was well-received, leading to more television appearances and eventually his own show, “Insomniac with Dave Attell.” The show, which ran for four seasons, was a hit and significantly boosted Attell’s popularity and net worth.

Continued Success in Comedy
