Johnny Brown cause of death, wife, children, net worth

American stage performer Johnny Brown has died. He died on Wednesday, March 2, 2022, at the age of 84.

Recognized for his role as the housing project superintendent Nathan Bookman on the TV show Good Times, Brown was a nightclub and stage performer as well as a comic actor, and a regular cast member of the television series Laugh-in.

Brown is mostly remembered for his portly physique, wide ingratiating smile, mobile facial expressions, and easy pleasant joking style.

Johnny Brown cause of death

Her daughter, actress Sharon Catherine Brown, announced the passing of the legendary actor via her Instagram page on Friday, March 4, 2022. In her announcement, she didn’t mention the cause of Johnny Brown’s death.

“Our family is devastated. Devastated. Devastated. Beyond heartbroken. Barely able to breathe,” Sharon noted in her Instagram post.

“We respectfully ask for privacy at this time because we need a minute to process the unthinkable. To articulate the depths of profound sadness. This is my mom’s husband for sixty-one years, mine and JJ’s dad, Elijah and Levi’s Pop-Pop, older brother to George and brother in law to Pat and extended family to Chris, Hihat, Damian and Derell.”

“It’s too terrible. It will never not be. It’s a shock. He was literally snatched out of our lives. It’s not real for us yet. So there will be more to say but not now. Dad was the absolute best. We love him so very much,” the statement added.

Was Johnny Brown married? Did Johnny Brown have children?

Johnny Brown was married to June Brown for 61 years. The couple brought forth two children; Sharon Brown and John Brown, Jr. As noted earlier, Sharon is an American actress.

Johnny Brown net worth

He had a net worth of $2 million at the time of his death.

When is Johnny Brown funeral? Date, time, venue, burial service, obituary poster

While announcing his demise on her Instagram page, Sharon didn’t shed light on Johnny Brown’s funeral and burial arrangements. She asked for privacy while mourning the late actor.
