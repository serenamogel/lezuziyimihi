Early Life & Biography

Mesut Ozil was born on 15 October 1988 in the city of Gelsenkirchen, Germany, to his parents Mustafa Ozil and Gulizar Ozil. His father, Mustafa, worked in the metal field as a worker, and his mother was a housewife. He spent his childhood in a village named Bismarck, which mostly consisted of immigrants. His childhood was spent through various hardships.

Ozil was influenced by football at a very early age and started developing skills to represent his school team. He played in his local football tournament named ‘Monkey Cage.’ He attended the school in Germany, which was also attended by his future national teammates, Manuel Neuer and Julian Draxler. He was signed by the Schale 04 youth team when he was only 17 years old.

Career

Ozil’s professional career started when he was signed by the youth club of F.C. Schalke 04 in 2006. He then moved to Werder Bremen in 2008, following a €5 million transfer fee. The same season, Werder Bremen finished second in the Bundesliga table. After showing incredible skills on the football pitch, he was then approached by one of the biggest clubs of the world, Real Madrid, and signed a €15 million deal with the club.

He joined the same shortly after the 2010 FIFA World Cup took place. Ozil wore the number 23 jersey of the club. In 2013, he made a move to the English Premier League and signed a £42.5 million contract with Arsenal F.C. In his debut season, he scored seven goals along with 13 assists in a total of 40 games. He lifted the Arsenal Player of the Season award in the 2015/16 season.

Ozil’s international career was successful as well, as he was the key player of the German National squad which lifted the 2014 FIFA World Cup, which took place in Brazil. He was also the golden ball nominee of the 2010 FIFA World Cup.
